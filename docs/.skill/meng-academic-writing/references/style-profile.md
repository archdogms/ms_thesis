# Meng Shuai Prior-Paper Style Profile

This profile is derived from the user's prior papers in `C:\Users\ms\Desktop\ms_thesis\docs\my_previous_papers`. Use it as a style guide, not as a source of facts or reusable wording.

## Corpus Observations

- Source texts include coursework papers on rural revitalization, Italian terrace gardens, brain signals and design evaluation, student movements in Republican China, and one presentation script.
- Most texts use a clear paper skeleton: title -> author/class information -> abstract -> keywords -> problem statement or main body -> literature review/case analysis -> conclusion.
- The writing is explanatory and problem-oriented. It tends to start from a broad context, introduce a practical or theoretical difficulty, then analyze causes, mechanisms, or examples.
- Sentence length is usually medium to long. Many sentences carry background, contrast, and conclusion in one flow.
- The style is more direct than ornate. It uses ordinary academic Chinese and occasional oral-like connective rhythm rather than dense theoretical jargon.

## Structural Habits

- Common headings: `一．摘要`, `二．问题提出`, `三．文献综述`, `四．实例分析`, `五．结论`, `总结`, `正文`.
- Arguments often follow:
  1. explain why the topic matters;
  2. point out the current problem;
  3. identify one or several causes;
  4. introduce a case, technology, person, or mechanism;
  5. return to broader significance.
- Conclusions often summarize what has been understood through the previous analysis, then connect it to a practical implication.

## Lexical Habits

Use these naturally, not mechanically:

- `对于...而言`, `对于目前存在的问题`
- `通过...可以看到/发现/了解到`
- `我们可以`, `我们需要`, `我们已经明白`
- `并且`, `但是`, `然而`, `同时`
- `这一`, `这种`, `相关`, `问题`, `机制`, `特点`, `意义`
- `在过去`, `当下`, `目前看来`, `从...来看`
- `一方面...另一方面...` for balanced evaluation

Prefer concrete phrases such as `造成...局面`, `形成...机制`, `起到...作用`, `提供...思路`, `带来...影响`.

## Reasoning Texture

- Use cause-effect language: `由于`, `从而`, `导致`, `因此`, `最终`, `由此形成`.
- Use modest evaluative language: `具有一定意义`, `在某些情况下`, `可能并不是那么有效`, `值得我们认真讨论`.
- Allow direct judgments, but ground them in the previous paragraph.
- Keep the prose readable for coursework and thesis contexts; avoid overly compressed theoretical abstraction.

## Academic Rigor Layer

The prior style sometimes contains strong claims and broad historical statements. For thesis-level work, keep the style but strengthen rigor:

- Add source markers for policies, dates, data, named scholars, and historical claims.
- Replace unsupported absolutes with qualified claims.
- Use `从现有材料来看`, `在一定程度上`, `可以认为`, `并不意味着` when evidence is partial.
- Keep citations and reference formatting consistent with the user's current thesis requirements.

## Micro-Revision Checklist

- Does the paragraph begin from context or phenomenon before moving to the claim?
- Is the central problem visible?
- Are `这一/这种/对于/通过/并且/但是` used only where they clarify relations?
- Is there at least one concrete noun or case anchoring each analytical paragraph?
- Are unsupported facts marked for citation rather than invented?
- Does the final version sound like a careful student paper, not a generic polished article?
